## **Why AGI-26**

The AGI Conference is the longest-running conference series in the world devoted wholly and specifically to the creation of AI systems with general, human-level intelligence. Now in its 19th edition, AGI-26 takes place July 27–31, 2026 at San Francisco State University, San Francisco, CA, the heart of the global AI industry.

The conference brings together 500+ attendees drawn from a 1,500+ global AGI researcher network, including 40+ speakers across keynotes, tutorials, demos, workshops, and panel discussions. The event also runs in hybrid format, with live-streaming extending reach to a global online audience. Past speakers have included Yoshua Bengio (Mila), Richard Sutton (University of Alberta), Jürgen Schmidhuber (IDSIA), Peter Norvig (Stanford), François Chollet (co-founder of ARC Prize), and Geordie Rose (founder of D-Wave and Sanctuary AI).

Critically, the conference is organized with deep involvement from SingularityNET and the ASI Alliance, both blockchain-native AI projects, meaning the audience is already familiar with and open to crypto-native approaches to AI infrastructure.
