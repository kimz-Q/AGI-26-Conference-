## Objective

Establish Qubic — and specifically **Neuraxon** and **Aigarth** — as credible, research-grade AGI projects in the eyes of the global scientific and technical community. Convert that credibility into developer relationships, research partnerships, and long-term ecosystem growth.
This is a relationship-building and opportunity-scoping exercise, not a deal-closing event. AGI-26 is a research forum. Our goal is to leave with qualified contacts, active dialogues, and a 90-day follow-up pipeline.

## Why AGI-26

AGI-26 is the 19th edition of the longest-running conference series devoted entirely to the creation of AI systems with general, human-level intelligence. It takes place **July 27–31, 2026** at **San Francisco State University**.

- **500+ attendees** drawn from a **1,500+ global AGI researcher network**  
- **40+ speakers** across keynotes, tutorials, demos, workshops, and panels  
- **Hybrid format** with live-streaming to a global online audience  
- Past speakers include **Yoshua Bengio** (Mila), **Richard Sutton** (University of Alberta), **Jürgen Schmidhuber** (IDSIA), **Peter Norvig** (Stanford), **François Chollet** (ARC Prize), and **Geordie Rose** (D-Wave / Sanctuary AI)  
- Organized with deep involvement from **SingularityNET** and the **ASI Alliance** — meaning the audience is already familiar with and open to crypto-native approaches to AI infrastructure

---

## Why This Matters for Qubic

Qubic is building **Neuraxon** and **Aigarth** — genuine AGI initiatives powered by Useful Proof of Work. Technical credibility must be earned in technical rooms. AGI-26 is that room.

**Legitimize Neuraxon and Aigarth** Presence at the world's premier AGI conference signals that Qubic's AGI roadmap is serious, peer-reviewed, and worthy of academic attention. This is a research forum, not a pitch event. That distinction matters enormously.

**Engage the researchers who define AGI** The 500+ attendees are scientists, engineers, and philosophers who write the papers, build the architectures, and set the standards by which all AGI projects are judged. One meaningful conversation here is worth thousands of impressions at a consumer event.

**Recruit technical talent and collaborators** Workshop attendees who engage with Qubic's UPoW model are exactly the kind of developers and academics who could contribute to, validate, or build on the Qubic stack.

**Bridge crypto and research communities** SingularityNET and ASI Alliance's involvement makes this conference uniquely receptive to blockchain-native AGI infrastructure. Qubic's participation advances the bridge between decentralized compute and frontier AI research.

**Create durable content assets** The video interview, post-event PR, and workshop materials produced here become long-lived assets — referenced by researchers, shared in academic communities, and used to support future fundraising and partnership conversations.

**San Francisco access** AGI-26 puts Qubic physically in San Francisco, surrounded by the investors, labs, and institutions that move the AI industry. The conference is the anchor. The surrounding access is equally valuable.

## Execution Plan

### 1 — Booth & Brand Presence

Staff the exhibitor booth throughout the 5-day conference with a focus on one-on-one conversations rather than passive brand exposure. Collateral will be research-oriented: technical explainers on UPoW, Neuraxon and Aigarth architecture, and the Qubic compute model — written for an audience that reads papers, not pitch decks.

### 2 — Keynote

David Vivancos and Dr. Jose Sanchez will deliver a 15-minute keynote on Qubic's UPoW approach to AGI computation and Neuraxon and Aigarth's development roadmap. Content is calibrated for a technical audience: architectural novelty, benchmarks, and open research questions. The presentation will be recorded and repurposed across Qubic's owned channels.

### 3 — Workshop

A dedicated Qubic technical session for self-selected researchers and developers. Deep-dive format: live demo of the Qubic compute model, technical Q&A on Neuraxon and Aigarth, and structured discussion on UPoW's role in AGI infrastructure. All attendees tracked for follow-up outreach and pipeline qualification.

### 4 — Media & Content

Pre-event content campaign announcing Qubic's participation, targeting both crypto and AI research communities. Conference video interview filmed for owned channel distribution. Post-event written recap and short-form video summarizing key outcomes, conversations, and next steps.

### 5 — Relationship & Pipeline Development

Structured 1:1 meetings with researchers, lab representatives, and investors identified in advance through Gold-tier matchmaking tools. All contacts and conversations captured in a structured follow-up pipeline for Ecosystem and BD teams.

### 6 — Follow-Up & Reporting

- Detailed report on the event outcome to be shared with the Strategic board.
- All captured contacts entered into CRM with track tags.
- Personalized outreach to priority contacts.
